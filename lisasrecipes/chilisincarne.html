Chili Sin Carne

  • 50Minutes
  • 4Serving

Ingredients

  • 1 large onion
  • 3 garlic cloves
  • 1 tablespoon of cumin powder
  • 1 teaspoon of chili powder
  • 1 sweet potato
  • 1 can chopped tomatoes
  • 1 can of corn
  • 1 can of red kidney beans
  • 200ml yogurt
  • 1 fresh / salad onion
  • 1/3 of a cucumber
  • olive oil
  • salt and pepper
  • 1 bag tortilla chips
  • 1 hand full of grated cheese
  • 75 gr rice per person

Method

  1. Chop the onion and 2 cloves of garlic into small pieces and add some olive oil to a medium large frying pan. When the oil is hot enough, fry the chopped onion and garlic on medium heat. Add the chili powder, the cumin powder and a pinch of salt. Let it simmer for 5 minutes.

  2. Peel the sweet potato and cut it into small pieces of 1cm by 1cm. Add the chopped potatoes in your pan and let them fry on medium heat for around 10 minutes. Make sure you don't let the ingredients stick to the pan, so keep stirring.

  3. While your potatoes are getting softer, you're going to prepare the fresh side dish. Chop the salad onions and cucumber into small pieces and mix this together with the yogurt in a bowl. Add 1 crushed clove of garlic and some salt, pepper and a bit of olive oil. Put aside and let the garlic do its job. Side dish is done!

  4. Your sweet potatoes have been baking for 10 minutes now, time to add the canned chopped tomatoes. Let it simmer on medium heat for about 15 to 20 minutes, until your potatoes are starting to get softer.

  5. In the mean time, boil some rice according to the method described on the package. Put some tortilla chips on a plate and sprinkle the grated cheese over it.

  6. When your potatoes are soften up, add the red kidney beans, the corn and half a cup of water. Let it simmer for 5 minutes and your chili is ready! Have a taste, and add more spices if you like. Put your plate with the tortilla chips in the microwave for 30 seconds and dinner is served!
